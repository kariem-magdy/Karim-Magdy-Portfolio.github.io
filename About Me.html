About Me

Hello! I’m Karim Magdy, currently pursuing my bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering as a Senior-1 student at Cairo University. I am a dedicated professional with a keen interest in data analysis and software development.

Recently, I completed a summer internship with Nokia, where I gained hands-on experience with cutting-edge technologies shaping the future of communication. The internship began with an extensive curriculum covering various topics, including the evolution from 2G to 3G, Nokia LTE Network (4G), 5G & IoT Radio Optimization, Data Analysis, and Power BI for data visualization. This foundational knowledge prepared me for the practical applications of these concepts.

Throughout my internship, I developed a deep understanding of 5G technologies, enhanced my analytical skills, and improved my ability to work collaboratively in a team environment. I also focused on mastering tools for data analysis and visualization, which are critical for optimizing resource allocation in telecommunications.

As the team leader for my project, I analyzed large datasets related to 5G network performance metrics, including signal strength, latency, and bandwidth utilization. My responsibilities included identifying trends and providing actionable insights to improve resource allocation strategies, ultimately contributing to enhanced network performance. This experience not only honed my technical skills but also reinforced the importance of data-driven decision-making in the evolving telecommunications landscape.

My core skills encompass data cleaning, transformation, dashboard creation, and report generation, all aimed at helping stakeholders make informed decisions. Additionally, I am eager to explore new technologies and expand my expertise in software engineering, data engineering, data science, and machine learning.
